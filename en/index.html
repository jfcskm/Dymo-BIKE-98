RIDER PROBLEMS

Small instability at the foot can become a major loss on the bike.

Knee discomfort after long rides

After long hours in the saddle, your knees may feel sore, unstable, or overloaded.

Power feels like it is leaking away

You push hard, but the force does not feel fully converted into forward momentum.

Unstable knee tracking

Your knees move side to side during pedaling, making your form less smooth and less efficient.

Fatigue in the second half of the ride

As fatigue builds, your pedal stroke becomes heavier and it becomes harder to maintain efficiency.

WHY IT MATTERS

Foot misalignment can reduce
pedaling efficiency.

When the foot and ankle are not stable, load balance during pedaling can become uneven. That instability may cause force to disperse instead of being directed cleanly into the pedal.

When the foot loses stability, pedaling force can become less direct and less efficient.

SOLUTION

Stabilize the foot.
Align the body. Transfer power.

Dymo® BIKE 98 helps guide the foot toward a more neutral position, supporting stable alignment from the foot to the knees, pelvis, and upper body.

POWER TRANSFER

A stable foot creates a more stable pedal stroke.

In cycling, efficiency is not only about strength. It is about how cleanly force travels through the body and into the pedal. Dymo® BIKE 98 supports the foot platform, helping riders maintain smoother alignment and reduce unnecessary movement.

In road cycling, performance is not determined only by how hard you push the pedals. What matters is how efficiently the force generated by the rider is converted into forward motion.

Because foot shape and alignment differ from rider to rider, the ideal insole is not the same for everyone. Dymo® BIKE 98 is based on the concept of a personal select insole: analyzing the foot and selecting the most suitable option from multiple insole variations.

As both a foot specialist and a cyclist, I believe that the starting point of bike fitting is not the frame. It is the feet.
