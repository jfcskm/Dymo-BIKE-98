PROBLEM

WHY

Poor foot alignment can cause
pedaling power loss

When the foot and ankle become unstable due to misalignment, weight distribution during pedaling becomes unbalanced. As a result, the force applied to the pedal may disperse instead of being efficiently converted into forward propulsion.

Loss of stability at the foot can shift load balance and make pedaling force more likely to disperse.

SOLUTION

Align the body from the feet up,
and turn every pedal stroke into forward power.

Dymo® BIKE 98 guides the foot toward a neutral position and supports a more stable riding form from the knees and pelvis to the upper body.

POWER TRANSFER

Foot instability can lead to
knee, pelvis, and upper-body movement

When the foot is unstable, pedaling force is lost as unwanted movement rather than converted into propulsion. Dymo® BIKE 98 helps stabilize the foot and supports smoother, more aligned pedaling through the knees, pelvis, and upper body.

In road cycling, what matters is not simply how hard you press the pedals, but how efficiently the force you generate is converted into forward propulsion. However, foot shape and alignment differ from person to person, which means the ideal insole is not the same for every cyclist.

Dymo® BIKE 98 is based on the concept of a “personal select insole,” where the condition of the foot is analyzed and the most suitable insole is selected from multiple options.

As a foot specialist and as a cyclist myself, I believe the starting point of fitting is not the frame, but the feet.
